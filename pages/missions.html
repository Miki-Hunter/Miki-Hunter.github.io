斯普特尼克 1 号 Sputnik 1Sputnik 1

1957 ● 已结束● Completed

人类历史上第一颗人造地球卫星，由苏联于 1957 年 10 月 4 日发射升空。这颗直径仅 58 厘米、重 83.6 公斤的金属球体，以每 96 分钟一圈的速度绕地球运行，向地面发送简单的无线电"哔哔"信号。Sputnik 1 的发射标志着太空时代的开启，直接引发了美苏太空竞赛，催生了 NASA 的成立。The first artificial satellite in human history, launched by the Soviet Union on October 4, 1957. This 58 cm-diameter metal sphere orbited Earth every 96 minutes, transmitting simple radio beeps heard worldwide. Its launch ignited the Space Race between the US and USSR, directly leading to the creation of NASA.

人类首次穿越小行星带并飞掠木星（先驱者 10 号）和土星（先驱者 11 号）的探测器。它们拍摄了第一批木星大红斑和土星环的近距离影像，为后来的旅行者号铺平了道路。两艘探测器各携带一块"先驱者镀金铝板"——刻有地球位置和人类形象的星际名片，是人类向宇宙发出的第一封"信件"。The first spacecraft to cross the asteroid belt and visit Jupiter (Pioneer 10) and Saturn (Pioneer 11). 维京号 Viking 1 & 2Viking 1 & 2

1975-1982 ● 已结束● Completed

人类首次成功在火星表面着陆并开展长期科学探测的任务。维京 1 号和 2 号各由一个轨道器和一个着陆器组成，传回了第一张火星表面彩色照片，并进行了著名的"标记释放实验"——测试火星土壤中是否存在微生物代谢活动。虽然实验结果至今仍有争议，但维京号奠定了人类对火星地质和气候的基本认识。The first successful Mars landing and long-term surface science mission. Each Viking consisted of an orbiter and a lander. They returned the first color photos from the Martian surface and conducted the famous Labeled Release experiment to test for microbial metabolic activity in Martian soil. Though the results remain debated, Viking established our fundamental understanding of Martian geology and climate.

旅行者号 Voyager 1 & 2Voyager 1 & 2

1977 ● 运行中● Active

人类历史上最伟大的探测任务。两艘探测器先后飞掠木星、土星、天王星和海王星，传回了前所未有的高清影像。2012 年，旅行者 1 号成为第一个进入星际空间的人造物体。它们各携带一张"金唱片"，记录着地球的声音与图像，作为给外星文明的问候。至今仍在向地球发送数据。Arguably the greatest exploration mission in human history. The twin spacecraft flew by Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune, returning unprecedented high-resolution images. In 2012, Voyager 1 became the first human-made object to enter interstellar space. Each carries a Golden Record — a time capsule of Earth's sounds and images, a greeting to any extraterrestrial civilization. Both still transmit data to this day.

伽利略号 GalileoGalileo

1989-2003 ● 已结束● Completed

人类首个木星轨道探测器，在木星系统工作了 8 年，完成了 34 次绕木飞行。伽利略号发现了木卫二（欧罗巴）冰壳下存在全球性液态海洋的关键证据，观测到木卫一（伊奥）上活跃的火山喷发，并释放了一枚大气探测器直接进入木星大气层。2003 年，伽利略号以坠入木星大气的方式结束了任务——避免污染可能存在生命的木卫二。The first Jupiter orbiter, which spent 8 years studying the Jovian system across 34 orbits. Galileo discovered compelling evidence for a global subsurface ocean on Europa, observed active volcanoes on Io, and deployed an atmospheric probe that plunged directly into Jupiter's atmosphere. The mission ended in 2003 with a deliberate plunge into Jupiter to avoid contaminating Europa.

1997-2017 ● 已结束● Completed

2004-2016 ● 已结束● Completed

人类历史上首次环绕并登陆彗星的任务。罗塞塔号飞行 10 年、跨越 64 亿公里，最终与 67P/丘留莫夫-格拉西缅科彗星交会。2014 年，菲莱着陆器成功降落在彗星表面——虽然因锚定系统故障而弹跳，但仍传回了宝贵的彗星表面数据。罗塞塔号首次在彗星上检测到甘氨酸（一种氨基酸），支持了"地球生命原材料可能来自彗星"的假说。The first mission to orbit and land on a comet. After a 10-year journey spanning 6.4 billion km, Rosetta rendezvoused with Comet 67P/Churyumov-Gerasimenko. In 2014, the Philae lander touched down on the comet's surface — the first comet landing ever achieved. Rosetta detected glycine, an amino acid, supporting the hypothesis that the building blocks of life may have been delivered to Earth by comets.

帕克太阳探测器 Parker Solar ProbeParker Solar Probe

2018 ● 运行中● Active

人类有史以来最接近太阳的探测器，其使命是"触摸太阳"。帕克号以每秒约 192 公里的速度（人类最快航天器）多次穿过太阳日冕——温度高达百万度的太阳外层大气。它使用 11.4 厘米厚的碳复合材料隔热罩抵御 1,400°C 高温，正在解开太阳科学的两大谜题：为什么日冕比太阳表面更热？太阳风是如何加速到超音速的？The closest-ever approach to the Sun — its mission is to "touch the Sun." Parker travels at ~192 km/s (the fastest human-made object) and repeatedly flies through the Sun's corona, where temperatures reach millions of degrees. A 11.4 cm carbon-composite heat shield protects it from 1,400°C temperatures. It is unraveling why the corona is hotter than the solar surface and how the solar wind accelerates.
